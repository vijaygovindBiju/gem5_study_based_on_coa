Key Conclusion

In a 2-Way Set Associative cache, we observe a significant performance gap between replacement policies. LRU (Least Recently Used) outperformed FIFO by reducing the miss rate from 1.79% down to 1.285%.

Unlike direct mapping, 2-way associativity provides two slots per set, allowing the hardware to make a choice during eviction. LRU's ability to preserve the most recently accessed data proves superior for the temporal locality present in our workload, resulting in a 4.2% reduction in CPI and a 9.1% faster Average Memory Access Time (AMAT) compared to FIFO.
